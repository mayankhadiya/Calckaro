What is Home Loan EMI and how is it calculated?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed monthly payment a borrower makes to a bank or housing finance company to repay a home loan. Every EMI has two components: principal repayment (which reduces your outstanding loan balance) and interest payment (the bank's charge for lending you the money).

In the early years of a home loan, the interest component dominates each EMI — sometimes up to 80-90% of the payment. As the loan matures, this ratio gradually flips: by the final years, almost the entire EMI goes toward principal. This is why prepaying early in the loan tenure saves significantly more interest than prepaying later.

Home Loan EMI Formula

The EMI is calculated using the standard reducing-balance formula:

EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n − 1)

Where: P = principal loan amount, r = monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n = total number of months (tenure × 12).

Tax benefits on Home Loan

Indian taxpayers can claim two main tax benefits on a home loan:

  • Section 80C — Repayment of principal up to ₹1,50,000 per year is deductible under Section 80C (along with other 80C investments).
  • Section 24(b) — Interest paid up to ₹2,00,000 per year is fully deductible from "Income from House Property" for self-occupied property. There is no upper limit if the property is let out (rented).

For first-time home buyers taking a loan up to ₹35 lakh (property value up to ₹45 lakh), an additional deduction of up to ₹1,50,000 on interest is available under Section 80EEA (extended in select budgets). Check the latest CBDT notification for current applicability.

Tips to reduce Home Loan EMI

  1. Increase down payment — A larger down payment means a smaller loan, lower EMI.
  2. Negotiate the rate — Even 0.25% lower rate saves lakhs over a 20-year tenure.
  3. Choose longer tenure cautiously — Longer tenure = lower EMI but much higher total interest.
  4. Prepay annually — Use bonuses, increments, or tax refunds to make partial prepayments.
  5. Transfer to lower-rate lender — Balance transfer can save 0.5-1% if you're on a higher rate.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is the fixed amount you pay to the bank every month towards repaying your home loan. EMI consists of two parts — principal repayment and interest payment.

How is home loan EMI calculated?

EMI is calculated using the formula: EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n − 1), where P is the principal loan amount, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the total number of months (tenure in years × 12).

What is the current home loan interest rate in India?

As of 2026, most Indian banks offer home loan interest rates between 8.40% and 9.50% per annum for salaried borrowers with good credit. SBI, HDFC, ICICI, LIC Housing Finance and PNB Housing Finance are the most common lenders.

What is the maximum home loan tenure in India?

Most Indian banks offer home loan tenure up to 30 years. The maximum is typically capped so that the borrower is no older than 65-70 years at the end of the loan tenure.

Is EMI fixed or floating?

Home loans in India are usually offered on floating interest rates, which means EMI changes when the bank revises its benchmark rate (repo rate linked). Some banks also offer fixed-rate loans for an initial 1-3 year period, after which they convert to floating.

Can I prepay my home loan?

Yes. Most banks allow prepayment of floating-rate home loans without penalty. Prepayment reduces your outstanding principal, which in turn reduces future interest outflow and shortens your loan tenure.
